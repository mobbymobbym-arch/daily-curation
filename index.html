Deep Analysis

Thin Is In (The Return of the Thin Client)

Summary: Ben Thompson argues that AI is bringing back the 'thin client' paradigm. Unlike the PC/Mobile era (thick clients), AI interactions (chat, agents) happen in the cloud. The local device's compute power matters less than its connectivity and the cloud's memory capacity.

Memory Crunch: A global memory shortage is being driven by AI demand (HBM for GPUs), making personal hardware (PCs, consoles) more expensive and less central to innovation.

Software Evolution: Software companies will use AI to write more code efficiently, but the 'per-seat' licensing model is threatened by the rise of AI agents. The battle will move to 'Work IQ' and agentic orchestration layers.

Microsoft Perspective: Microsoft is prioritizing its own AI (Copilot) over Azure rental capacity due to supply constraints, aiming to own the 'Work IQ' layer of identity and artifacts.
